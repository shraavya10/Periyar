The event that impacted Ramasamy's life
Kasi Pilgrimage Incident In 1904, E.V. Ramasamy went on a pilgrimage to Kasi to visit the revered Shiva temple of Kashi Vishwanath. Though regarded as one of the holiest sites of Hinduism, he witnessed immoral activities such as begging, and floating dead bodies. His frustrations extended to functional Hinduism in general when he experienced what he called Brahmanic exploitation. However, one particular incident in Kasi had a profound impact on E.V. Ramasamy's ideology and future work. At the worship site there were free meals offered to guests. To E.V. Ramasamy's shock, he was refused meals at choultries and monasteries , which exclusively fed Brahmins. Due to extreme hunger, E.V. Ramasamy felt compelled to enter one of the eateries disguised as a Brahmin with a sacred thread on his bare chest. The gatekeeper at the temple concluded that E.V. Ramasamy was not a Brahmin. He not only prevented Periyar's entry but also pushed him rudely into the street. As his hunger became intolerable, Periyar was forced to feed on leftovers from the streets. Around this time, he realised that the eatery which had refused him entry was built by a wealthy non-Brahmin from South India. In the pilgrimage, he even saw a female beggar who was selling herself for food. He also found that the priests cheated the pilgrims of their money and did not perform the religious rituals as required. This discriminatory attitude dealt a blow to Periyar's regard for Hinduism, for the events he had witnessed at Kasi were completely different from the picture of Kasi he had in mind, as a holy place which welcomed all.Ramasamy was a theist until his visit to Kasi, after which his views changed and he became an atheist.
Periyar after coming back to his home was given the full responsibility of managing and running the family business. Periyar began to help people out in many ways. Though he lacked formal education, he knew about law and its loopholes and weaknesses. Whenever a dispute occurred, he was the judge of it . If people wanted roads, they went to Periyar. He indulged in many activities to help his people .

The unfolding of events.


Year 1905 Onwards
One noteworthy instance: Once the dreadful contagious disease plague attacked Erode. Hundreds of people died and thousands fled for saving their lives. But this noble man did not desert his native town like other rich merchants. He helped the people go to hospitals and he paid for their treatment. He himself carried the dead bodies on his back to the cremation ground.
FRIENDSHIP OF TAMIL SCHOLARS 1905-06 :In his youthful years he was attracted by the Tamil scholar Pandithamani Ayothidhaasar who vehemently condemned the caste - system and Hindu religion of Brahmins with the principles of rationalism and Buddhism. There was a Tamil Scholar by name: PULAVAR MARUDHAIYA PILLAI at Karur. His logical arguments and daring condemnation of Hindu religion, caste-system, deceptive myths in the epics and Vedic shastras (Hindu Dogmas) spread by the Brahmins, had attracted admiration of even the ordinary rural people in the areas around Erode and Karur. His friendship played a major role in implanting atheism[no belief in God] firmly in the mind of Periyar.
Year 1909
Periyar arranged the remarriage of his sister’s daughter who became a child widow at the age of 9.Hence it was a widow remarriage
Year 1910
He became a member of the Erode Municipal Council.
He was also the chairman of the Erode Devasthanam which was a temple board. Though he was an atheist, he performed his duties without letting personal feelings come in the way. He was a person rooted for his people knowing their sentiments and their feelings.He ran it successfully.
around 1917 or 1918
Elected Chairman of Erode Municipality and held a responsible position in Erode Taluk Temple Committee and many other honorary posts for several years. During his tenure as the Municipal Chairman, drinking water scheme was implemented . The functioning of the public sanitation department under him was excellent and efficient. He Joined Madras Presidency Association (MPA) floated by the non-Brahmin Congress leaders to counter the Justice Party. Elected Vice-President of MPA and served as the Reception Committee Chairman for its second provincial conference held at Erode in October 1919 He held many honorary positions like the President, the Secretary, Vice-President etc., in various public institutions numbering 29 such posts. E.V. Ramasamy joined the Indian National Congress in 1919
1919-1920
Resigned from the Chairmanship of Erode Municipality in protest against the Jallianwala Bagh massacre and after brief tie up with Annie Besant's Home Rule movement, joined Indian National Congress soon to be elected President of Tamil Nadu Congress Committee. From 1920 onwards he was consistently urging the Tamil Nadu Congress Committee to demand for proportional representation of non Brahmins and depressed castes in government services.
Periyar trusted and accepted Mahatma Gandhi as his leader. So, as a true disciple he began to execute every word of Gandhi into deed. One such example is Khadhi yarn spun by hand with Chakra. As soon as the command of Gandhi for wearing Khadhi cloth was announced, Periyar immediately shed all his costly foreign clothes and began to wear Khadhi. Also he forced all the members of his family to wear khadhi only. He held the chairmanship of Erode Municipality and wholeheartedly undertook constructive programs spreading the use of Khadi, picketing toddy shops, boycotting shops selling foreign cloth, and eradicating untouchability.
Year 1920
Actively involved in Gandhi’s non cooperation movement and Constructive Programme together with his wife Nagammal and sister S.R.Kannammal.
Year 1921 There was the Temperance Movement headed by Mahatma Gandhi to boycott toddy shops. It was part of the Non Cooperation Movement. This was done by picketing the toddy shops. Periyar Ramasamy too took part in this movement. He owned a grove of five thousand coconut trees and he ordered the grove to be cut down. He was arrested for picketing the toddy shops. When he was in jail, his wife Nagammal and his sister Kannamma continued with his work.
